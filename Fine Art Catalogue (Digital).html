Re-viewing the Gaze

Exhibition Catalogue

For this exhibition, the viewer is invited to walk around the exhibit and view each piece of imagery and attempt to recognise what the artwork presents in terms of Gender, Sex, and

Sexuality. Does the artwork present these concepts as distinct from one another, blend into one another, or act as synonymous to one another?

After this, the spectator is again invited to view the exhibit, but now with the red-tinted eyewear provided and view the artwork in a new perspective. Does anything new become

apparent, does the conversation around the distinctiveness or synonymous nature of Gender, Sex, and Sexuality become clearer or less so?

It is the hope of this exhibition that through the fundamental participation of the viewer,

new discussions can be had not just about how we see Gender, Sex, and Sexuality but upon

how we can reflect on how we contextualise what we see in general. Exposing the often over- simplified nature of social discourse which inevitably leads to exclusion, discrimination and spread of misinformation, and disinformation.

About the artist.

Jo Flint graduated from Loughborough University with a BA Fine Art degree before being

awarded a ABF Fellowship and going onto study an MA in Graphics Design & Visualisation again at Loughborough University on Scholarship. Gender and discussing Social Inequality in the

Arts was something that steered her work ever since her Foundation year at Batley Collage of Arts in which she would begin her journey into Portrait Photography.

This exhibition was influenced by her profound influential MA tutors who guided her to utilise her Fine Arts background with complementing Graphical Design techniques.
